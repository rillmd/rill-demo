Monday morning. First day of the async standup experiment.

Honestly, a little nervous about it. The whole premise is that writing things down will be lower overhead than a 15-minute Zoom, but I'm not sure that's true. Writing is *harder* than talking for a lot of people — you have to pick words, you worry about tone, you wonder if the thing you typed sounds dumb. Talking is low-friction; you just say the thing.

My worry: we trade 15 minutes of synchronous meeting for 30 minutes of everyone staring at a text box deciding how to phrase "still working on UUID migration, no blockers."

But I also want to give it a real shot. Two-week trial, not a one-day gut check. If it fails, I want it to fail for a real reason we can point at.

Going to write mine first so Alex and Jordan have a template to react to. Keep it short: what I shipped yesterday, what I'm doing today, blockers. Resist the urge to turn it into an essay.

Check in with myself on Friday: did it actually save time, or did it just move the cost around?
