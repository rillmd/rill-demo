Alex's UUID v7 vs ULID comparison doc just landed in my inbox. Reading through it over coffee.

The verdict is UUID v7, and honestly I think it's the right call. Three reasons stand out:

1. Ecosystem support — Postgres 17 has native v7 handling coming, and most of our client libraries already treat UUIDs as a first-class type. ULID would mean custom codecs in every SDK.
2. Timestamp-prefixed ordering — we get the same "index-friendly" property as ULID, which was the whole reason we were tempted by ULID in the first place.
3. Nothing to explain to new engineers. "We use UUIDs" is a sentence that ends the conversation. "We use ULIDs because..." is a meeting.

The one thing I still want to push on: the monotonicity guarantee within a single millisecond. Alex's doc waves at it but doesn't spell out what happens under burst traffic. Going to bring it up in standup.

Funny how much of this decision comes down to boring-but-correct. Staff-engineer energy is mostly about resisting the shiny thing.
