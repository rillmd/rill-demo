## Async Standup Proposal

The current synchronous daily standup is inefficient for a distributed team. 20 minutes of round-robin status reporting with limited engagement. Timezone spread (14-hour gap with Jordan Kim) makes scheduling painful.

### Problems with Current Format

1. Status reporting consumes the entire meeting -- no time for meaningful discussion
2. Timezone constraints force suboptimal meeting times
3. Half the team disengages during others' status updates
4. Status information is better captured by existing project tools

### Proposed Format

- **Async daily updates**: Everyone posts a brief update (blockers, decisions needed, highlights) in a shared channel by their local morning
- **Bot-generated digest**: Automated collection and summary of all updates
- **Weekly sync meeting**: Focused exclusively on blockers and multi-person decisions. Not status reporting

### Expected Impact

- Save approximately 80 minutes per week (4 x 20-minute standups eliminated)
- Better timezone accommodation
- Higher signal-to-noise ratio in the remaining weekly sync
- Shift standup purpose from "report status" to "surface blockers and align"
