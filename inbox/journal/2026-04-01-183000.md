The weekly standup format is not working great for our remote setup. We spend 20 minutes going around the virtual room with "yesterday I did X, today I'll do Y" and half the team zones out. The timezone spread makes synchronous standups painful anyway -- Jordan is 14 hours ahead.

I think we should try an async standup format. Everyone posts a brief update in a shared channel by their morning. A bot collects them and generates a digest. We keep the synchronous meeting but make it weekly and focused on blockers and decisions, not status updates.

The key insight from that blog post I read is that standups should surface blockers and create alignment, not report status. Status is better tracked through the tools we already use. What if we focused the sync meeting entirely on "what's blocking you?" and "what decisions need multiple people?"

Going to draft a proposal for this. Could save the team 80 minutes a week.
