End of day one on async standup. Reflecting.

Surprisingly pleasant. Jordan's write-up was way more detailed than anything that would ever come out in a verbal standup — they included a short paragraph on why the batch error schema was harder than expected, with a concrete example of an edge case around partial success. That kind of substance basically never surfaces in a 15-minute round-robin because by the time it's your turn to talk, you compress everything to "still working on batch errors, tricky edge cases, nothing blocking." The depth was only possible in writing.

Downside I noticed: slower to catch blockers that need *immediate* discussion. Alex flagged something about the migration script that I would have pushed back on in real time, but in async it took me four hours to notice and respond. We lost half a day.

Maybe the right pattern is async writes + a short sync slot reserved for "anything needs real-time discussion?" Not a full standup, just a 5-minute escape valve.

Too early to judge. But the detail in Jordan's post alone is worth something. Going to keep going.
