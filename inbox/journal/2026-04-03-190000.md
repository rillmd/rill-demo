1-on-1 with Alex this afternoon, mostly about the database migration timeline for v2.

The thing that stuck with me: Alex always frames risk in terms of "what happens at 3am when this breaks." It's such a simple reframe, but it forces you to think about operability before elegance. I was leaning toward a clever dual-write strategy with automatic reconciliation, and Alex basically said "okay, now imagine you're on call, it's 3am, reconciliation has diverged — what's your runbook?" I didn't have one.

We ended up sketching a simpler approach: freeze v1 writes during a maintenance window, backfill, then flip. Less sexy, but the 3am story is two paragraphs instead of ten.

I want to internalize this. So many of my design instincts are shaped by what reads well in a design doc, not what survives an incident. Going to start asking myself the 3am question before I commit to any migration plan.
