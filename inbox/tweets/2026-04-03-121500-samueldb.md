UUID v7 is quietly becoming the de facto standard for new APIs in 2026. If you're still reaching for v4 by default, you're leaving real wins on the table.

Timestamp-ordered IDs mean your inserts land near each other in the B-tree. Better page locality, fewer random writes, happier databases. The fact that you also get a free "created at" for debugging is just gravy.
